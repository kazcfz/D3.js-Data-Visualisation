Mental Health Issues in Tech Industries




Workers living with Mental Health Issues

Currently (2016)

Had it previously (2016)

Currently: Number of workers who were living with Mental Health Issues that year.
2018 had the highest record of workers with Mental Health Issues at 46.1% compared to the lowest record, 2016 at 40.1%
Although the number of workers with Mental Health Issues in 2019 has dropped to 42.1%, the number of unsure workers reached its highest at 29.1%

Had it previously: Number of workers who have been living with Mental Health Issues prior that year.
2016 had the highest percentage of workers who have had Mental Health Issues previously at 53.8%. Fortunately, the numbers have dropped to its lowest in 2019 at 42%.
However, while the percentage of the unsure were at its lowest in 2016 at 15.2%, it was in its highest in 2019 at 28%.









Workers have ever been diagnosed with Mental Health Issue (2016-2019)

Records of workers' answer to the question, "Have you ever been diagnosed with a Mental Health Issue?".
2016 had nearly 50% on workers who have been diagnosed and those that have not. However, a large majority were diagnosed workers from 2017 onwards.
On top of that, the number of workers who have answered to this question has drastically dropped from 1433 to 322 during 2016 to 2017, and gradually decreased from then onwards.

Workplaces Formally Discusses Mental Health Issues: Records of workers' answers to the question, "Has your employer ever formally discussed mental health?"
2016 had the lowest percentage of workplaces which performed Formal Discussions at 20.1%. As years pass, the 'Yes' answers had been constantly increasing to its highest in 2019 at 34.2%.

Workplaces provides Mental Health Benefits: Records of workers' answers to the question, "Does your employer provide mental health benefits as part of healthcare coverage?"
2016 had the lowest percentage of workplaces which provided Mental Health Benefits at 46.3% and the higehst 'Not eligible / NA' answers at 7.2%.
Until 2018, where the percentage of 'Yes' answers were at its highest at 59%, it has been constantly growing. However, in 2019, the percentage of 'Yes' answers has reduced whereas the 'I don't know' answers increased.











Types of Mental Health Issue diagnosed in workers

From the whole of 2016-2019, Mood Disorder affected the most workers, next to Anxiety Disorder.
2016 had the highest number of mentally ill workers for every listed disorder, except for Psychotic Disorder, which only affected its most in 2017.
Psychotic Disorder had also affected the least workers overall, next to Dissociative Disorder.

Workers with Mental Health Issues worldwide (2016)

In 2016, United States of America had the highest number of mentally ill workers (survey participants) worldwide with 840 workers, next to United Kingdom with 190 workers, Canada with 78 workers, Germany with 58 workers, Netherlands with 48 workers, and Australia with 35 workers.
